Minor Scales

The natural minor scale is one of the most popular scales used nowadays. How is it composed? What are the other types? That's what we're going to see right now.

1. Definition

Pop artists use a lot the natural minor scale - which is also called Aeolian mode -, especially when writing a sad song, giving some melancholy to it, some darkness.

It's a diatonic scale, composed of seven notes, 5 whole-steps and 2 half-steps (also known as tones and semitones). The eighth note is the octave. Here's the A Natural Minor Scale, which is the easiest to write:

A - B - C - D - E - F - G

2. Interval and degrees

As mentioned before, every natural minor scale is divided into seven notes, which have different intervals between them. It goes like this:

W - H - W - W - H - W - W

W means Whole-Step (or tone) and H, Half-Step (or semitone). Every note represents a certain degree, from I to VII (in Roman numerals). It means that for the A Minor scale, A is the first degree, and G the seventh. Pretty logical. Let's see the name of each degree:

  • I: the Tonic
  • II: the Supertonic
  • III: the Mediant
  • IV:  the Subdominant
  • V: the Dominant
  • VI: the Submediant
  • VII: the Subtonic
  • VIII: the Octave
